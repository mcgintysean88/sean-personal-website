seanmcginty.me · Brand Guidelines v1.0

The visual language of a curious marketer.

This document defines the colors, typography, spacing, and principles that make seanmcginty.me feel like a considered, human, and quietly sophisticated place.

Informed by: jopecuro.com (motion & scale), brentstirling.com (directness & personality), jasonfaber.ca (warmth & structure)

01 — Color System

Warm, confident, considered.

A light-mode palette anchored by warm parchment and deep navy. One teal accent does most of the work — used for links, highlights, and interactive states. Warm amber appears sparingly for moments that need energy.

Background #F8F5F0 Page background, warm parchment
Text Primary #141E30 Headlines, body copy, primary text
Text Secondary #5C6878 Subheads, captions, supporting copy
Text Muted #9AA3AF Labels, dates, fine print
Accent Teal #1A7A78 Links, CTAs, hover states, highlights
Accent Light #E5F4F4 Hover backgrounds, tags, subtle fills
Accent Warm #C87941 Italic cycling text, emphasis, energy moments
Dark #0A1628 Hero section, dark backgrounds, footer
Border #E2DDD6 Dividers, card borders, rule lines

04 — Spacing

Give things room to breathe.

Generous spacing is one of the clearest signals of a confident, well-considered design. When in doubt, add more space rather than less.

--space-xs 8px Icon gaps, tight pairs
--space-sm 16px Between label and heading
--space-md 24px Card padding, element gaps
--space-lg 48px Between components, grid gaps
--space-xl 80px Sub-section separation
--space-2xl 120px Section padding (standard)
--space-3xl 160px Hero padding, major sections

Layout rules

Max content width 1,100px

Centered, padded 48px on sides

Body text max width 660px

For readable paragraphs (~65–70 chars/line)

06 — Interactions & Motion

Motion as meaning.

Animations should feel smooth and purposeful — never decorative for its own sake. Motion signals that this site was built with intention, which is the whole point.

I've spent 13 years making marketing
smarter.

↑ Signature interaction: cycling words animate on a 2.5s loop. This is the first thing visitors see.

Cycling Headline

The hero headline cycles through 4 words — "smarter", "faster", "more human", "harder to ignore" — on a fade + slide animation. This is the signature creative element borrowed from jopecuro.com.

animation: fadeSlide 2.5s ease
transform: translateY(10px → 0)

Scroll Reveal

Sections and major content blocks fade in and slide up slightly as they enter the viewport. Use an IntersectionObserver with a 100ms stagger for child elements.

opacity: 0 → 1
transform: translateY(24px → 0)
transition: 500ms ease
stagger: 100ms

Hover States

All interactive elements have subtle hover states. Links gain a teal underline. Cards lift slightly. Buttons shift one shade darker. Never abrupt — always 200ms ease.

transition: all 200ms ease
links: color → var(--accent)
cards: box-shadow lift

Smooth Scroll

Navigation links scroll the page smoothly to sections. This is set at the HTML level with scroll-behavior: smooth and reinforced with scroll-margin on section anchors.

html { scroll-behavior: smooth }
section { scroll-margin-top: 80px }

07 — Voice & Tone

Sound like a person, not a profile.

The copy on this site should read like Sean wrote it — warm, direct, smart, with a bit of wit. Not like a resume. Not like a LinkedIn headline. Borrowed from brentstirling.com's personality-first approach.

✓ Do

"I like problems that seem complicated until they're not."

Specific, personal, shows a way of thinking

"We protected unit economics. We kept partner relationships intact."

Active, short, result-oriented

"I'm in the early stages of building something around that. More on that soon."

Honest and forward-looking without overselling

✗ Don't

"Results-driven marketing executive with a passion for leveraging data-driven insights."

Jargon-stuffed, sounds like a resume

"I help brands unlock transformative growth through innovative AI-powered solutions."

Buzzwords, says nothing specific

"Thought leader, innovator, and change agent with 13+ years of experience."

Self-congratulatory and generic

08 — Photography

Natural, not staged.

The existing headshot sets the right tone: genuine smile, clean shirt, neutral background — approachable but polished. Any additional photography should maintain that standard.

☀️

Natural light preferred

Avoid harsh studio lighting. Soft, diffused natural light reads as warm and authentic.

🎯

Neutral or textured backgrounds

The existing headshot's muted concrete wall is ideal. Avoid busy backgrounds or distracting environments.

👤

Genuine expression

Candid > posed. A real smile beats a forced one every time. The site is personal — the photo should be too.

🌊

Charleston context

If location shots are used (lifestyle, context), Charleston's coastal character is a natural fit — low-key, not clichéd.

🎨

Consistent color treatment

If photos are edited, apply a consistent warm-neutral grade. Avoid heavy filters or high-saturation edits.

📐

Headshot usage

Primary use: About section, large and prominent. Can appear smaller in the hero or nav. Do not crop tightly — give the photo breathing room.
